1. Mexican Gothic by Silvia Moreno-Garcia

Set in 1950s Mexico, this atmospheric gothic novel follows a glamorous young woman named Noemí who travels to a remote, crumbling mansion after receiving a frantic letter from her newlywed cousin. She uncovers a sinister, mushroom-rooted menace and a controlling, eugenicist family hiding a dark legacy of horror.

2. The Final Girl Support Group by Grady Hendrix

A clever, slasher-inspired horror thriller about the ultimate survivors of real-life massacres. For years, they have been meeting in a secret therapy group. When one of the women is suddenly murdered, the rest realize someone is hunting down the final girls one by one, forcing them to fight back.

3. Incidents Around the House by Josh Malerman

A terrifying, modern psychological horror novel told from the perspective of an eight-year-old girl named Bela. She begins talking to and fearing a menacing, unseen entity she calls "Other Mommy" who lives inside her walls and demands to be invited in.
